Application Areas

While some still see augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) as technologies reserved for hardcore gamers, many have taken notice of the considerable potential for each across a variety of industries.

As hardware developers make AR and VR headsets more affordable and user-friendly, startups are developing new use cases far beyond gaming — applying the technologies to everything from marketing to healthcare to space exploration, and more.

Medicine

Augmented reality can reduce the risk of an operation by giving the surgeon improved sensory perception.
Students use the technology to practice surgery in a controlled environment.
The technology can be combined with MRI or X-ray systems and bring everything into a single view for the surgeon.

Construction

AR can be used for design analysis to pick out clashes by virtually walking through your completed model.
It fits the bill for constructability review by letting the architect and contractor collaborate on changes that have to happen between design and construction due to constructability issues

Education

AR has the potential to change the location and timing of studying, to introduce new and additional ways and methods.
It can allow professionals to conduct training in a virtual environment where they can improve upon their skills without failing the operation.

Real Estate

Real estate professionals are successfully using VR to help their clients not only envision, but digitally tour homes and properties from anywhere in the world
by simply wearing a VR headset and entering a lifelike simulation of that property.
